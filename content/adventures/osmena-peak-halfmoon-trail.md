Every tribe has an origin story. Ours begins on the Halfmoon Trail.

On January 7, 2017, three friends — **Edward, Ronald, and Ariel** — decided to climb Osmeña Peak, the highest point on Cebu island, taking the long way: the traverse from Badian known as the *halfmoon trail*. What was supposed to be a simple day hike turned into something more — hours on the trail talking about the mountains we still wanted to see, and the realization that we didn't want this to be a one-time thing.

By the time we stood among the jagged hills of Mantalongon, looking out over the southern coast of Cebu, Tribu Siga was born.

## Why "Siga"?

In Cebuano, *siga* means flame — and it's also slang for someone tough, someone fearless. We liked both meanings. A fire that keeps burning, and the nerve to keep climbing.

From that day on, the rule was simple: keep the flame lit, leave no trace, and always help each other up the mountain — just like the two climbers in our logo.
