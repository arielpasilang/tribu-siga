Ask any Filipino mountaineer to name the hardest climb in the country and you'll hear the same two syllables twice: **Guiting-Guiting**. The name means *jagged* — and the mountain is honest advertising.

Rising straight out of the sea on Sibuyan Island, G2 is less a hike than a three-day scramble: the infamous **Knife Edge**, the false summits of Mayo's Peak, and long stretches where the trail is really just a rock face with opinions.

## The summit register

Our summit photo tells the story — six of us crowded around the battered *G2 Summit* sign in full rain gear, soaked, grinning, clouds sealing off the view completely. We didn't care. You don't climb Guiting-Guiting for the view; you climb it to find out if you can.

We could. Fifteen months after three friends walked up Osmeña Peak, Tribu Siga had climbed the most technical mountain in the Philippines.
