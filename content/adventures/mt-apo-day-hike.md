The highest point in the Philippines. In one day.

Most groups take three or four days to climb **Mt. Apo** — at 2,954 meters, it's the grandfather of all Philippine mountains, a massive stratovolcano looming over Davao. We decided to try it as a day hike: start in the dark, move light and fast, summit by midday, and get back down before our legs figured out what we'd done to them.

## The push

The route throws everything at you — farm trails, mossy forest, the endless boulder field where sulfur vents steam out of the rocks, and finally the crater rim where the whole of Mindanao spreads out below.

Standing on the roof of the Philippines with the tribe's flag was the proudest moment of our first year. Every training hike, every BMC session, every rainy Negros traverse had been building to this.
