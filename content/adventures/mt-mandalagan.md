Mandalagan doesn't get the fame of Kanlaon, its louder neighbor to the south — and that's exactly why we loved it.

Rising above the old wartime village of Patag in Silay, Mt. Mandalagan is a sprawling, ancient volcanic complex of hidden valleys, sulfur vents, and grass clearings that open suddenly out of dense forest. For three days we walked through some of the wildest terrain in northern Negros.

## The valley camp

The picture we kept from this climb says it all: packs lined up in a wide green clearing, sheer forested walls rising on every side, everybody sorting gear before the night's camp. Standing in that valley you feel properly small — in the best way.

Mandalagan tested our rain systems, our knees, and our sense of humor. It passed all three back to us in better shape than it found them.
