Our first major expedition as Tribu Siga, and still one of the most beloved.

Mt. Talinis — the *Horns of Negros* — is the second-highest mountain on Negros island, an old volcano wearing a crown of mossy forest and hiding a string of crater lakes on its shoulders. For four days we hiked through cloud forest, pitched camp beside the still waters of the twin lakes, and learned what it really means to carry everything you need on your back.

## The photo by the lake

The group shot by **Lake Nailig** from this trip became our banner picture for years — the whole tribe in matching shirts, the forested crater wall rising behind us, clouds rolling over the rim.

It was on this climb that we wrote the line that still defines us:

> "In every mountain we climb, we create wonderful memories, meet awesome friends, and a thousand stories to tell."

Talinis gave us all three.
